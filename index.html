Akhilesh Kumar Ramachandran

PhD Candidate  ·  Sport and Health Sciences  ·  Cardiff Metropolitan University

I am a PhD candidate in Sport and Health Sciences at Cardiff Metropolitan University, specialising in sports biomechanics, strength and conditioning, and injury prevention. My research uses systematic review and meta-analytic methods to synthesise evidence on neuromuscular training, plyometrics, and lower-limb biomechanics — with a focus on ACL injury risk in youth athletes. I also investigate performance determinants in cricket and the clinical utility of surface electromyography in musculoskeletal conditions.

Research

ACL Injury Prevention in Youth Female Athletes

Biomechanics Injury Prevention Systematic Review

Investigating how neuromuscular training alters jump-landing biomechanics and ACL injury risk across maturation stages in female youth athletes. Combines systematic review, meta-analysis, and biomechanical outcome measures.

Plyometric Training & Athletic Performance

Plyometrics Strength & Conditioning Meta-analysis

Synthesising evidence on the effects of plyometric and combined training on strength, power, speed, and balance across different athlete populations including youth soccer players and gymnasts.

Cricket Biomechanics & Performance

Sports Biomechanics Cricket Performance Analysis

Identifying biomechanical and physical determinants of fast bowling speed and batting performance in cricket using novel correlational meta-analytic frameworks and task-constraints approaches.
