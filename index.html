Applied Research Automation 2022 – Present

Markerless Motion Capture Validation

Background

Traditional marker-based motion capture is accurate but time-intensive and lab-dependent. Markerless systems offer scalability for clinical and field-based use, but require rigorous validation before adoption.

Aim

To determine the real-world accuracy and product readiness of a markerless motion capture system (Theia3D) across a range of dynamic movements, benchmarked against a gold-standard marker-based system.

Methodology

30+ participants tested simultaneously using Theia3D (markerless) and Vicon (marker-based). Reliability and validity of key joint angles analysed in R and MATLAB. Automated processing pipelines built to handle 1,500+ trials, reducing processing time by 70%.

Research Biomechanical Modelling 2022 – Present

ACL Injury Prevention in Youth Female Athletes

Background

ACL injuries are among the most serious in youth sport, with female athletes at significantly higher risk. The mechanisms linking maturation and movement patterns to injury risk remain poorly characterised at a population level.

Aim

To identify knee injury risk patterns in athletes using biomechanical modelling and translate statistical findings into actionable screening and prevention strategies.

Methodology

Large-scale quantitative studies with 100+ participants. Biomechanical modelling applied to motion capture data to quantify knee loading across dynamic movement tasks. Statistical analyses conducted in R and SPSS, with findings mapped to clinical screening tools.

UX Prototyping Applied Technology 2022 – Present

VR Kinematic Feedback System

Background

Athletes typically receive movement feedback after the fact, through video review or verbal coaching. Real-time feedback during movement has potential to accelerate skill acquisition and reduce injury risk, but applied VR tools in sport science remain limited.

Aim

To design and deploy a VR-based system using Meta Quest 3 that delivers real-time kinematic feedback during dynamic athletic tasks, and measure its effect on movement performance.

Methodology

End-to-end prototype design and deployment using Meta Quest 3. Kinematic data captured during movement and fed back in real time through VR overlays. Tested across 30+ participants on dynamic movement tasks, with pre/post performance measures collected and analysed in MATLAB and R.

Data Science Wearable Sensors 2021 – 2022

GPS & Wearable Athlete Monitoring

Background

Managing athlete workload across a long competitive season is critical for performance and injury prevention. Wearable sensor data offers a high-resolution, individualised view of athlete load, but requires robust data pipelines and modelling to be actionable.

Aim

To monitor and model physical load across an elite football season using GPS and MEMS sensors, and build a validated predictive tool to support training and recovery decisions for coaching staff.

Methodology

Wearable MEMS-GPS sensors deployed across 20+ elite football players for a 48-week competitive season. Over 400 longitudinal athlete records cleaned and processed in MATLAB and R, covering physical load, cardiovascular intensity, sprint velocity, and acceleration. Regression modelling used to identify key performance predictors and build personalised workload models.

Research Performance Analysis 2022 – 2024

Cricket Biomechanics & Performance

Background

Fast bowling speed is a key performance determinant in cricket, yet the biomechanical and physical factors that predict it had never been systematically quantified using correlational evidence across the literature.

Aim

To identify biomechanical and physical determinants of fast bowling speed in cricket and translate findings into evidence-based strength and conditioning recommendations for coaches and practitioners.

Methodology

A novel correlational meta-analytic framework was developed and applied to the cricket biomechanics literature. Systematic search conducted across major databases. Correlational data pooled and analysed in R and SPSS. Findings mapped directly to strength and conditioning programming principles.
