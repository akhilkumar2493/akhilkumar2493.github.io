Accuracy of AI-Powered Motion Capture: A Validation Study

Markerless Motion Capture Validation · Cardiff Met · 2022–Present

30
Participants
4
Jump Tasks
0.76–0.96
ICC (Sagittal Plane)

Snapshot

Problem

Markerless motion capture systems like Theia3D need reliability evidence before clinical adoption — especially in youth female athletes where maturation drives trial-to-trial variability.

Approach

Examined within-session reliability of kinematics and kinetics during 4 jump-landing tasks in 30 youth female athletes across 3 maturity groups using Theia3D and Kistler force plates.

Impact

  • Sagittal plane ICC of 0.76–0.96 — good to excellent reliability across tasks and limbs
  • SEM below 5° for frontal and transverse planes — sufficient precision for ACL screening
  • Pre-pubertal group showed highest measurement error — maturity-adjusted thresholds recommended
  • Time-series ICC analysis identified landing phase regions of high variability

Process

  1. 1
    Recruited 30 youth female athletes classified into pre-pubertal, pubertal, and post-pubertal groups by percentage of predicted adult height
  2. 2
    Captured 3 trials of CMJ, DVJ, SLCMJ, and mSLDVJ using an 8-camera Theia3D system at 100 Hz, synchronised with Kistler force plates at 1000 Hz
  3. 3
    Processed kinematics in Visual3D with a 14 Hz filter; landing phase normalised to 101 data points for time-series analysis
  4. 4
    Analysed relative reliability (ICC3,1) and absolute reliability (SEM) at discrete time points and across the full landing phase waveform in R and SPSS
Tools: Theia3D · Visual3D · Kistler Force Plates · R · SPSS

Biomechanical Risk Modelling and Injury Prediction

Biomechanics & Injury Prevention · Cardiff Met · 2022–Present

100+
Participants
2,400+

2
Publications

Snapshot

Problem

ACL injuries disproportionately affect youth female athletes — yet the maturation-related biomechanical mechanisms driving this risk are not well understood at a population level.

Approach

Combined a systematic review (2,400+ papers) with a large-scale motion capture study (100+ athletes) to model knee injury risk across maturation stages and translate findings into screening tools.

Impact

  • Post-puberty identified as the highest-risk window for ACL injury biomechanics
  • Neuromuscular training significantly reduced knee valgus and improved landing mechanics
  • Findings translated into evidence-based injury risk screening protocols
  • Published in Sports Medicine 2024 and 2025

Process

  1. 1
    Reviewed 2,400+ papers across major databases in a systematic review of ACL injury biomechanics and maturation
  2. 2
    Recruited 100+ youth female athletes classified by maturity status for a cross-sectional motion capture study
  3. 3
    Collected 3D kinematic and kinetic data during jump-landing tasks, quantifying knee loading across planes of motion
  4. 4
    Modelled biomechanical risk factors using regression analysis in R and SPSS; validated against clinical screening tools
Tools: Motion Capture · Biomechanical Modelling · R · SPSS

Snapshot

Problem

Athletes typically receive movement feedback after the fact. Real-time feedback during the task itself has potential to accelerate skill acquisition and reduce injury risk — but applied VR tools in sport science remain limited.

Approach

Designed and deployed an end-to-end VR system on Meta Quest 3 that streams live kinematic data as in-headset overlays during dynamic athletic tasks, tested across 30+ participants.

Impact

  • 10% measurable improvement in movement performance across participants
  • Real-time kinematic feedback delivered successfully during dynamic tasks
  • VR validated as a scalable, practical feedback tool for applied sport science
  • Manuscript currently in preparation

Process

  1. 1
    Designed a VR prototype on Meta Quest 3 with a real-time pipeline connecting motion capture output to in-headset display
  2. 2
    Captured kinematic data via Theia3D during dynamic movement tasks and streamed it as live visual overlays
  3. 3
    Tested across 30+ participants using pre- and post-performance measures on standardised dynamic tasks
  4. 4
    Analysed performance outcomes in MATLAB and R to quantify feedback-driven movement improvements
Tools: Meta Quest 3 · Theia · MATLAB · R

Wearable Sensor Analytics for Performance Prediction

GPS & Wearable Athlete Monitoring · Univ. of Los Lagos, Chile · 2021–2022

Snapshot

Problem

Managing athlete workload across a long competitive season is critical for performance and injury prevention — but without robust data pipelines and validated models, coaching decisions rely on intuition.

Approach

Deployed MEMS-GPS sensors across 20+ elite footballers for a full 48-week season, processed 400+ longitudinal records, and built personalised workload models to predict performance outcomes.

Impact

  • Workload and maturity status explained 55% of performance variance
  • Personalised models improved training individualisation and recovery decisions for coaching staff
  • Published in Healthcare (2022) and Human Movement (2022)

Process

  1. 1
    Deployed MEMS-GPS sensors across 20+ elite footballers at Universidad de Los Lagos, Chile for a full 48-week competitive season
  2. 2
    Collected physical load, sprint velocity, cardiovascular intensity, and acceleration data across all training sessions and matches
  3. 3
    Processed 400+ longitudinal athlete records in MATLAB and R, cleaning and structuring data for modelling
  4. 4
    Built regression models to identify key performance predictors; validated personalised workload thresholds for coaching use
Tools: MEMS-GPS Sensors · MATLAB · R · Regression Modelling

Evidence-Based Optimisation of Human Performance

Cricket Biomechanics & Performance · 2022–2024

Novel
Analytic Framework
2
Publications

Background

Fast bowling speed is a critical performance determinant in cricket, yet the biomechanical and physical factors that predict it had never been systematically quantified using correlational evidence across the literature. Coaching decisions were largely based on intuition rather than data.

Aim

To identify biomechanical and physical determinants of fast bowling speed in cricket and translate findings into evidence-based strength and conditioning recommendations that coaches and practitioners can act on.

Methodology

A novel correlational meta-analytic framework was developed and applied to the cricket biomechanics literature. A systematic search was conducted across major databases. Correlational data was pooled and analysed in R and SPSS, and findings were mapped directly to strength and conditioning programming principles.

Results

Hip-shoulder separation and run-up velocity were identified as the strongest predictors of bowling speed. The novel framework was validated and subsequently adopted in related research. Published in Sports Biomechanics (2024) and International Journal of Sports Science & Coaching (2022).

Tools: R · SPSS · Correlational Meta-analytic Framework
